**_The best mirin substitutes have both acidity and sweetness, including: sweet Marsala wine, dry white wine, dry sherry, and rice vinegar._** These won’t be as sweet, so try **adding 1/2 teaspoon of sugar per tablespoon**. If you prefer to avoid cooking with alcohol, **Mizkan Mirin-style seasoning is a non-alcoholic option**.

**Where can you find mirin?**

**Japanese specialty markets and grocery stores carry both hon-mirin and aji-mirin.** If you don’t have a Japanese or Asian grocer nearby, or you’re looking for authentic mirin, a small-batch Japanese mirin brewed by the Sumiya family in the coastal city of Hekinan is available on Amazon. Brands like Kikkoman are widely available and can be found in grocery stores across the United States.
